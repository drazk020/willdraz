Early Life

Born in Minneapolis, Minnesota, I developed an early passion for both technology and finance. Growing up with a Dad who actively invested, he took me under his wing to learn how to passively invest. I was exposed to business concepts from an early age. My fascination with computers began when I build my first PC at age 13, sparking what would become a lifelong interest in technology and its applications in business.

High School Years

During high school at Onalaska High, I excelled academically while actively participating in the Future Business Leaders of America (FBLA) and founded the Investment Club at my school. As captain of the soccer team, I developed strong analytical and public speaking skills. These experiences solidified my interest in pursuing a dual major in Finance and Mathematics.

Present Day

Currently in my second year at the University of Minnesota Duluth, I'm maintaining a 3.5 GPA while working in sales and operating a business on behalf of my parents. I specialize in developing financial analysis tools and implementing enterprise systems. Recently, I led a successful project that automated financial reporting processes, reducing processing time by 60%. My unique blend of financial acumen and technical expertise positions me well for a career at the intersection of finance and technology.

Personal Interests

Hobbies

I'm an avid programmer who enjoys building financial analysis tools in Python. In my free time, I contribute to open-source projects and participate in hackathons. I also enjoy chess and am currently rated 1800+ on chess.com.

Sports & Fitness

An active tennis player and runner, I participate in local tennis tournaments and have completed three half-marathons. I practice yoga three times a week and enjoy hiking in Minnesota's state parks.

Arts & Culture

I'm a classical music enthusiast with a particular interest in piano compositions. I regularly attend performances at the Minnesota Orchestra and enjoy reading financial literature and technology publications. My favorite authors include Michael Lewis and Walter Isaacson.

Individual Research

Current Research

I'm currently researching the application of machine learning algorithms in financial risk assessment. My work focuses on developing predictive models for market volatility using Python and TensorFlow, incorporating both traditional financial metrics and alternative data sources.

Publications & Presentations

Recently presented findings on automated trading algorithms at the Minnesota Finance Conference. Collaborating with faculty on a forthcoming paper about AI-driven risk management systems.

Future Directions

Future research will focus on developing decentralized finance (DeFi) applications and studying their impact on traditional banking systems. I'm also keen to investigate the role of quantum computing in financial modeling.
